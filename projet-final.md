## Guidelines

Each team of 2-4 people should **select one article** from the provided list to read and analyze. The goal is to create a clear, concise presentation based on the article. Up to 2 teams can select the same paper.

Additionally, each team should **implement an algorithm** described in the article and conduct a small experiment. The experiment should be rather short and involve creating one or two plots to explore specific experimental dimensions.

The expected deliverables are an **oral presentation** (10min) and **a report** (up to 6 pages, but less than 6 is fine and won’t be penalized as long as it is enough to cover what you did).

**Mandatory:** disclose your usage of AI Assistants in a dedicated section.


**Some questions you may use to guide your reading are (among others):** 

* Did you like the paper? Did you find it interesting? Be honest\!  
* What are the most important things you learned from the paper? Why are they important?  
* Do the lessons learned generalize beyond the specific task? Do they contribute towards building an important system or application?  
* Is the experimental setup satisfying? Any experiments missing? Any obvious or important baseline missing?  
* Is the problem/approach well motivated?  
* Are you convinced by the results? Why?  
* Is the writing clear? Is the paper well structured?


### Project Goal

Each team will work on a selected research article from the provided list. The project consists of three main tasks:

1. **Understand and Analyze the Paper**  
   Read the selected article carefully. Identify its main contributions, methodology, experiments, and results. Think critically about its strengths and weaknesses.

2. **Implementation and Experiment**  
   Select **one algorithm or method described in the paper** and implement it. Design a **small-scale experiment** to test or explore it. This should be lightweight but meaningful, e.g., reproducing a key result or testing the method under new conditions. You should produce **figures** to illustrate your findings.

3. **Technical report**
   Write a technical report on your analysis of the paper and your own experiments.

The project is not about reproducing the entire paper, but rather demonstrating your understanding by engaging with one part of it in a practical way.

### Experiment Guidelines

As part of the project, each team is required to implement one algorithm or method described in the paper and run a small experiment. The purpose is not to reproduce the entire experimental section, but to explore one aspect of the method in a practical and insightful way.

#### Scope and Expectations

- Select a **specific algorithm or method** from the paper.
- Design a **small-scale experiment** that:
  - Reproduces a key result, or
  - Tests the method under new or simplified conditions (e.g., fewer data, smaller model).
- The experiment should produce **figures or plots** that illustrate your findings.
- The goal is to demonstrate understanding and critical thinking, not large-scale reproduction.
- Simple, well defined and well presented experiments are preferred over extensive but messy experiments.


#### Resource Considerations

- We are fully aware of your **computational limitations**. 
- **You are not expected to run large models** such as GPT-3.5, GPT-4, or LLaMA-70B.
- Instead, you can:
  - Use **smaller models** to test whether the approach scales down effectively.
  - Investigate the method's behavior in a **low-data regime** (e.g., few-shot learning).
  - Explore **model quantization or compression** techniques to fit larger models within resource constraints.
    - Many tutorials (e.g., on Hugging Face) explain how to run quantized models on limited hardware like Google Colab GPUs.


#### Report guidelines

- Maximum length: **up to 6 pages** (less than 6 pages is acceptable, as long as the content sufficiently covers the work done).
- This is a **scientific report**. It should clearly describe:
  - What you understood from the paper.
  - What you implemented.
  - The experiments you ran and the results you obtained.

The report **must include** the following sections:

- **Contributions Section**  
  Clearly outline your original contributions, including:
  - Implementation details
  - Experimental design and results
  - Any novel ideas or modifications
- **Limitations Section**  
  Provide critical reflections on:
  - Limitations or weaknesses of the original article
  - Limitations of your own implementation and experiments

If either the Contributions or Limitations section is missing, the grade will be **penalized**.

- **Visual Presentation**  
  The clarity, structure, and formatting of the report (including use of plots, figures, and layout) **will be evaluated**.
